Okro Soup

Published by Amaka Orji, May 14, 2023

Prep and Cooking Time about 30 minutes

List of Ingredients

Okra: Fresh or frozen works for this recipe. You can find frozen okra in the frozen section of most grocery stores. Animal Protein: I used shrimp, dried catfish, and smoked mackerel. Please see the substitutes below. Crayfish: this is sun-dried shrimp that is used in most Nigerian dishes. It adds so much flavor to this okra stew and shouldn't be substituted. Here is a link to grab yours. Greens: I used fresh kale for this dish. Other great substitutes are spinach, amaranth, Kale, or any green vegetable. Feel free to omit the greens if you don't have them handy. Stock: beef, chicken, vegetable stock, or broth. Even better, use the leftover stock from the cooked meat. Seasoning: habanero peppers and ground black pepper. Seasoning cube is optional, but it adds a great flavor to the dish since there isn't much time to build flavor.


Steps

Preparation

1. Divide the okra in half. Place one-half in the food processor. Add the habanero, onion, and fresh ginger. Give it a few pulses until it reaches your desired consistency. Set aside

2. Finely chop the other half. Set it aside for later.

3. Bring broth to a boil on medium heat. Add the dried catfish, cook uncovered, then remove the fish. Pull apart the head of the fish and remove any bones, leaving only the part with flesh.

4. Add the okra from the food processor, shrimp, crayfish, and seasoning cube into the pot with broth and cook uncovered.

5. Add the cleaned dry fish and chopped bell peppers. Cook for a few minutes.

6. ChAdd the sliced okra with chopped kale, and stir to combine. Cook for a minute.

7. Add the smoked mackerel and fold in the okra soup, careful not to break the fish. Cook for a minute or so. Remove from heat and serve with pounded yam, eba (garri), or any swallow of choice.
